Cookie Policy

Effective: 26 March 2026 · Last updated: 26 March 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how P247 (p247.io) uses cookies and similar technologies on our website. It does not apply to the P247 iOS app, which does not use cookies.

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the site remember your preferences, understand how you use it, and improve your experience. Some cookies are placed by us ("first-party") and some by services we use ("third-party").

3. Cookie Categories

Essential Cookies

Required for the website to function properly. These include security cookies from Cloudflare that protect against bots and malicious traffic. You cannot opt out of essential cookies because the website will not work without them.

Analytics Cookies

Help us understand how visitors use our website (which pages are visited, how long people stay, where traffic comes from). We use Google Analytics 4 with the following privacy settings:

  • IP anonymisation is enabled by default in GA4
  • We do not enable Google Signals (no cross-device tracking)
  • We do not link GA4 data to any personally identifiable information
  • Data retention is set to 14 months
  • We do not use GA4 data for remarketing or advertising

Marketing Cookies

We do not use marketing, advertising, or tracking cookies. We do not run retargeting campaigns or share cookie data with ad networks.

4. Your Choices

Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View cookies currently stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites or all sites
  • Block third-party cookies while allowing first-party cookies

Instructions for common browsers:

  • Chrome
  • Firefox
  • Safari
  • Edge

Google Analytics Opt-Out

You can opt out of Google Analytics across all websites by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Do Not Track

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. There is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT. We respect DNT signals by not loading analytics cookies when a DNT header is detected.

5. Consent (EU/UK Users)

If you are visiting from the European Union or United Kingdom, we will ask for your consent before setting non-essential cookies (analytics). Essential cookies are loaded without consent as they are strictly necessary for the website to function.

You can withdraw your consent at any time by clearing your cookies or using the cookie preference link in our website footer.

6. Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use:

  • Local Storage: to store preferences (e.g., early access form submission state) on your device. This data does not leave your browser.
  • Server logs: standard web server logs that record IP addresses, user agents, and request URLs for security and debugging purposes. Logs are retained for 30 days.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy when we add or remove cookies or tracking technologies. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision.
