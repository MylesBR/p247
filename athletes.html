You track everything. HRV. Sleep. Recovery scores. Training load.

But the most important signals for performance aren't tracked by any wearable.

The 6 blind spots in your data

1
Muscle damage vs nervous system recovery. HRV bounces back in 24 hours. Muscle tissue takes 48 to 96. Your green score doesn't mean your legs are ready for heavy squats.
2
Nutrition and glycogen depletion. Your wearable has no idea what you ate. Calorie deficit and low glycogen tank performance regardless of recovery score.
3
Training monotony and overtraining risk. Same intensity every day. Your watch sees volume but not variety. High monotony with high load is the fastest path to breakdown.
4
Psychological fatigue. Work stress triggers the same cortisol response as a hard session. Your recovery score can't tell the difference between a bad week at work and overtraining.
5
Injury compensation patterns. Favouring one side. Altered gait. Subtle movement changes. Your wearable tracks output but not quality of movement.
6
Fragmented athlete data. Five apps, zero synthesis. The biggest blind spot is that nothing connects your wearable, nutrition, and body comp data together.
